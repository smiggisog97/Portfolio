## Design Context

### Users
Hiring managers at product-led companies and tech startups. They scan portfolios fast — within 30 seconds they're forming a verdict. They've seen hundreds of designer portfolios. They're looking for signal: precision, taste, confidence.

### Brand Personality
Calm authority. Quietly exceptional. Soothing without being forgettable.
Three words: **still · precise · assured**

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial minimalism — the kind found in a well-designed monograph or a high-end architecture firm's site. Nothing shouts. Everything is placed with intention. White space does the heavy lifting. Typography carries the personality. Motion is slow and deliberate — never performative.

Anti-reference: busy agency portfolios with scroll-jacking, parallax overload, neon accents, and "look how creative I am" energy. This is the opposite of that.

### Design Principles
1. **Silence is a design choice.** Empty space signals confidence. Don't fill it.
2. **Typography IS the design.** Instrument Serif carries the voice. Every size, weight, and spacing decision is load-bearing.
3. **Motion should feel inevitable.** Reveals should feel like something settling into place — not a trick.
4. **Nothing competes.** One thing at a time deserves attention. Hierarchy is enforced by restraint, not decoration.
5. **Details are the product.** A hiring manager who notices the scrollbar, the underline animation, the letter-spacing — that's the moment the portfolio works.
